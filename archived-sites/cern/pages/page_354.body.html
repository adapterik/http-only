RFC sites

This is a list made in Feb 92 from Joyce K. Reynolds 's rfc-retrieval.txt of sites from which Internet RFCs (and often many other things) may be obtained. Use one close to you! If you don't know which RFC you want, use the full text index.

Primary repositories in the USA

RFCs are obtianable from the following sites:
SRI
RFCs
NSF
RFCs etc (A VM system - requires GUEST as password so not www browsable right now),
JvNCnet
RFCs
ISI
RFCs (but FTP server prevents browsing directories!),
Concert
RFCs. Also has gopher and wais (db name?) servers;
WUSTL
Compressed RFCs. At WUARCHIVE.WUSTL.EDU the RFCs are in an "archive" file system and various archives can be mounted as part of an NFS file system. Please contact Chris Myers (chris@wugate.wustl.edu) if you want to mount this file system in your NFS.
DDN
DDN NIC.

There is also a primary archive site in the UK. Secondary repositories follow in alphabetical order by country.

Austrailia and Pacific Rim

RFCs available from munnari. Contact: Robert Elz &lt.kre@cs.mu.OZ.AU&gt.

Denmark

RFCs are available from the University of Copenhagen .

Germany

University of Dortmund: RFCs in compressed format

Finland

FUNET has RFCs in compressed format . Other interesting directories covering internet-drafts, useful documents, source for Internet networking applications, etc. Also provides email access by sending mail to archive-server@funet.fi.

Netherlands

EUnet has RFCs in compressed format and also many other interesting directories covering IETF, internet-drafts, the Internet Resource Guide, shadows of other files on NIC.DDN.MIL, shadows of files on NIS.NSF.NET, etc.

Norway

RFCs are available from UNIT .

Sweden

RFCs are available from sunet , and chamers . The NORDUnet NIC has no RFCs, but other interesting documents, including: Drafts of standards, other documents "pre-RFCs" NSFNET Linkletters , Notes from IETF working groups , and the Internet Resource Guide

UK

Compressed RFCs are available from Imperial College, a primary archive site.

USA

RFCs available from cerfnet (contact: help@cerf.net).
